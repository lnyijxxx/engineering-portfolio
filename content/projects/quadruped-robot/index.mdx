## Overview

A four-legged robotic platform designed and built from individual components.
The robot uses a Raspberry Pi 4 and eight LX-16A serial bus servos to explore
embedded control, inverse kinematics, and autonomous locomotion.

## Goal

Create a modular robotics testbed that can progress from reliable joint control
to coordinated walking, ROS 2 integration, vision, and autonomous navigation.

## Architecture

- Raspberry Pi 4 running Python control software on embedded Linux, configured
  for headless SSH access from a laptop without a dedicated monitor or keyboard
- USB-to-TTL interface connecting the Pi to the serial servo bus
- Eight LX-16A servos controlling the hip and knee joints
- Separate regulated power rails for the servos and onboard computer

## Hardware

- Raspberry Pi 4 Model B
- 8x LX-16A serial bus servos
- 12 V, 3000 mAh battery and LM2596 buck converter
- 3D-printed chassis and custom wiring harness

## Software

- Python servo communication and control
- MATLAB leg simulation and inverse-kinematics validation
- Raspberry Pi OS and UART serial communication

## Engineering challenge

Designing a stable electrical architecture for eight servos while maintaining
reliable Raspberry Pi operation under changing loads. The physical integration
also needed to remain serviceable and leave room for future sensors.

## Engineering decision

Validate the leg kinematics in MATLAB before commanding the physical hardware.
This creates a safer, measurable transition from geometry to joint angles and
reduces the risk of damaging the mechanisms during early development.

## Future development

- Complete inverse kinematics and stable walking gaits
- Integrate ROS 2 and an onboard camera
- Add IMU-based stabilization and autonomous navigation
