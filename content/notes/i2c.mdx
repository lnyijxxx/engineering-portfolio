## What it is

Inter-Integrated Circuit - a two-wire (SDA/SCL) synchronous bus supporting
multiple devices via 7-bit (or 10-bit) addressing, with open-drain lines that
require pull-up resistors.

## Common failure mode: bus lockup

If a device is interrupted mid-transaction (power glitch, MCU reset, sleep
mode) it can be left holding SDA low, which locks the entire bus for every
device on it - not just the offending one. Fix is a manual clock-out routine:
toggle SCL a handful of times to force the stuck device to release SDA, then
reissue a start condition.

## Pull-up sizing

Too weak (high resistance) and rise time suffers at higher bus speeds; too
strong (low resistance) and you waste current and can exceed a device's sink
current rating. 4.7k is a reasonable default for 100kHz on short buses.
