## The three terms

- **P** - proportional to current error, the main driver of response speed
- **I** - accumulates past error, eliminates steady-state offset, but adds
  lag and can cause overshoot/windup if not clamped
- **D** - reacts to rate of change of error, dampens oscillation but
  amplifies sensor noise

## Tuning approach

Start with I and D at zero, increase P until the system oscillates, back off
to ~50-60% of that value, then add D to damp remaining oscillation, then a
small amount of I only if there's persistent steady-state error.
