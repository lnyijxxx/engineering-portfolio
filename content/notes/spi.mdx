## What it is

Serial Peripheral Interface - a synchronous protocol using a shared clock
(SCLK), separate data lines for each direction (MOSI/MISO), and a
per-device chip-select (CS) line.

## Why use it over I2C

Higher throughput (no address overhead, full duplex), simpler slave-side
implementation. Tradeoff is more pins - one CS line per device versus I2C's
shared two-wire bus for many devices.

## Clock polarity and phase (CPOL/CPHA)

The most common source of "device doesn't respond" bugs - the four SPI
modes (0-3) determine which clock edge data is sampled on. Always check the
datasheet's timing diagram, not just the mode number, since naming
conventions aren't perfectly consistent across vendors.
