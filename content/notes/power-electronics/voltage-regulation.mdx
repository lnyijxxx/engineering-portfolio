## Linear (LDO) vs switching

LDOs are simple and low-noise but dissipate the voltage difference as heat -
fine for small current draws (like an MCU at 3.3V from 5V), impractical for
larger drops or currents. Switching regulators are efficient across a wider
range but introduce switching noise that can matter for analog-sensitive
circuits nearby.

## Dropout voltage

An LDO needs some minimum headroom between input and output to regulate
correctly - check this against your actual supply voltage, not just the
nominal one, since a slightly low input (e.g. a discharging battery) can push
you under dropout before you'd expect.
